Short bio

Brian Mboya is a Senior Software Engineer at Soapbox Inc. He studied computer science at DeKUT University in Kenya. He is a volunteer at TReND, teaching African scientist the Python programming language to help them use programming in their research and application of machine learning techniques. He attended summer school for Computational Neuroscience and Machine learning basics by TReND in Accra, Ghana. He is scheduled to attend the IBRO-Simons Computational Neuroscience Imbizo by Imbizo Africa.

Interests

I am fascinated by the human brain and how it works. My fascination with the brain grew as a result of studying computation in machine. My interest is to understand how the brain works computationally, and my main area of study is attention, specifically visual attention in both humans and animals. This is in hopes the studying attention will gives us clues to how, where and why consciousness emerges in the brain.

My research questions include:

  • Is visual attention fundamental to memory retention?
  • Is there any correlation between visual attention and attention required to complete a cognitive task?
  • What is the neural correlate of attention?
  • And how does attention relate to dreams?
